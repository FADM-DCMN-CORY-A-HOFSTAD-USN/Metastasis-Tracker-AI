# Metastasis-Tracker-AI: Core Academic, Government, and Military References

The underlying biological modeling, fractal scaling, and legacy mainframe interoperability schemas utilized within the Metastasis-Tracker-AI suite are grounded in the following clinical research and federal architectural frameworks.

## 1. Metastatic Hematogenous Dissemination & Lymphatic Egress
The predictive tracking of variant species via vascular and lymphatic routes relies heavily on established clinical pathology regarding hematogenous intravasation and tumor margin routing.

* **Lymphatic or Hematogenous Dissemination: How Does a Metastatic Tumor Cell Decide?**
    * *Source:* National Center for Biotechnology Information (NCBI) / PubMed Central (.gov)
    * *Summary:* Details the biological mechanics of how migratory cells detach from the primary tumor and intravasate into blood vessels or lymphatics. It confirms that sheer volumetric pressure (up to ~4 × 10⁶ cells released per gram of primary tumor daily) overcomes metastatic inefficiency, validating the mass-projection mechanics of our `population_engine.py`.
    * *Link:* https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC1459485/
* **Lymph Metastasis Mechanics & VEGF-C Pathways**
    * *Source:* Steele Labs, Harvard University / Massachusetts General Hospital (.edu)
    * *Summary:* Analyzes the functional lymphatic vessels in the margins of tumors and their active role in attracting tumor cells. Demonstrates that tumor cells can be visualized moving in lymphatic vessels in novel **variant species** models, directly supporting the chemokine affinity modeling mapped in `target_proteins.py`.
    * *Link:* https://steelelabs.mgh.harvard.edu/lymphatics/metastasis

## 2. WBE Fractal Scaling & Anatomical Generation
The `patient_anatomy.py` engine utilizes the West, Brown, and Enquist (WBE) scaling laws to mathematically construct the 31 generations of the human arterial tree.

* **A General Model for Metabolic Scaling in Self-Similar Asymmetric Networks**
    * *Source:* Department of Ecology and Evolutionary Biology, University of Arizona / PubMed (.gov / .edu)
    * *Summary:* Explores the application of the WBE model beyond symmetric branching, analyzing positive and negative asymmetry in cardiovascular networks. Validates the $B \propto M^{3/4}$ metabolic scaling core assumption used to map baseline hemodynamic resistance based on patient dimensions.
    * *Link:* https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5378416/
* **Sizing Up Allometric Scaling Theory**
    * *Source:* National Institutes of Health (NIH) (.gov)
    * *Summary:* Provides the analytical framework for finite-size corrections to the 3/4 exponent in mammalian cardiovascular systems, governing how the fluid viscosity matrix processes the pressure drop across our capillary beds.
    * *Link:* https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/18787686/

## 3. Legacy Mainframe (Univac) & Global FHIR Interoperability
To track oncological data globally, the suite bridges legacy military/healthcare mainframes into modern FHIR R4 interoperability standards via the Univac-IX daemon.

* **Federal Interoperability Standards Platform (ISP) - FHIR Ecosystem**
    * *Source:* Office of the National Coordinator for Health Information Technology (ONC) (.gov)
    * *Summary:* Defines the exact FHIR R4 (Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources) Core Components required by federal regulations to break down data silos. Validates our `DiagnosticReport` and `Observation` JSON transaction bundles used for global ingestion.
    * *Link:* https://isp.healthit.gov/fhir-ecosystem-0
* **Evolution of Military Health Systems (MHS) & Legacy Mainframes**
    * *Source:* U.S. Military Entrance Processing Command (USMEPCOM) (.mil)
    * *Summary:* Documents the transition of global military health tracking from early IBM 370/165 mainframes and UNIVAC System 80 microcomputer links into the modern, fully consolidated MHS GENESIS electronic health record platform. This hardware evolution provides the historical foundation for deploying our Univac-IX security bridge to modernize and sanitize legacy health data inputs securely.
    * *Link:* https://www.mepcom.army.mil/About-Us/History/
* **Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources (FHIR) IG Creation Process**
    * *Source:* National Library of Medicine / PubMed (.gov)
    * *Summary:* Outlines the "Infrastructure as Code" methodologies, utilizing CI/CD pipelines and FHIR Shorthand to securely route and map electronic health records on a global scale.
    * *Link:* https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/42322886/
